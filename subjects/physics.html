Definition Contest - Physics

1. Define speed as distance travelled _____

Key: per unit time

2. Define velocity as _____ in a given _____

Key: speed, direction

3. A vector quantity has both _____ and _____

Key: magnitude, direction

4. Define acceleration as change in _____ per unit _____

Key: velocity, time

5. Weight is a _____ force on an object that has _____

Key: gravitational, mass

6. Define gravitational field strength as _____ per unit _____

Key: (gravitational) force, mass

7. Define density as _____ per unit _____

Key: mass, volume

8. Define the spring constant as _____ per unit _____

Key: force, extension

9. Hooke's law: the extension of the spring is (directly) proportional to the force acting on it, _____

Key: within the limit of proportionality

10. Define the moment of a force as moment = force × _____ from the pivot.

Key: perpendicular distance

11. Two conditions for an object in equilibrium: _____ is zero and _____ is zero.

Key: resultant force, resultant moment

12. Define momentum as mass × _____. Don't accept speed

Key: velocity

13. Define impulse as force × _____

Key: time (for the force acts)

14. Define resultant force as the rate of change in _____

Key: momentum

15. Magnitude of centripetal force depends on object's mass, _____

Key: (rotation) speed/radius (of the circular path)

16. Define power as the rate of _____

Key: doing work/work done/energy transferred

17. Define pressure as _____ per unit _____

Key: force, area

18. Define specific heat capacity as the _____ per unit mass to raise the temperature of a substance per unit _____

Key: energy required, temperature (change)

19. Define evaporation as the _____ particles overcoming the attraction force and escaping from the _____ of the liquid.

Key: more energetic, surface

20. Transverse wave: the direction of vibrations is _____ to the direction of propagation.

Key: perpendicular

21. Longitudinal wave: the direction of vibrations is _____ to the direction of propagation.

Key: parallel

22. The normal line divides the angle between the _____ and the _____ into two equal angles.

Key: incident ray, reflected ray

23. The angle between the _____ and the _____ is known as the angle of incidence.

Key: incident ray, normal

24. The angle between the _____ and the _____ is known as the angle of reflection.

Key: reflected ray, normal

25. Define ultrasound as sound with a _____ higher than _____

Key: frequency, 20 kHz

26. If the temperature stays constant, the _____ of the gas is inversely proportional to its _____

Key: pressure, volume

27. Define critical angle as the angle of _____ when angle of refraction is 90°

Key: incidence

28. Metal can conduct electricity easily because it has _____

Key: free (moving) electrons

29. State what is meant by electromotive force (e.m.f.):

A The force supplied to move electrons.

B Energy supplied in driving unit charge around a complete circuit.

C The driving force provided by electric motor.

D The current induced by electromagnetism.

Key: B

30. _____ can protect the appliance when the current is too high.

Key: Circuit breaker/fuse

31. _____ can be used to avoid electric shock when touching the metal case.

Key: Earthing (wire)/connecting to earth

32. Iron, cobalt, _____, and steel are four common magnetic metals.

Key: nickel

33. Permeant magnets are _____ to be magnetized and _____ to lose magnetism.

Key: hard/difficult, hard/difficult

34. The direction of magnetic field points from _____ pole to _____ pole.

Key: north, south

35. To determine the force acting on a wire with current in a magnetic field, Fleming _____ rule can be used.

Key: left hand

36. Define Monochromatic as light of _____

Key: a single [frequency/wavelength]

37. Alpha particle is a _____ nucleus.

Key: helium

38. Gamma ray is a high energetic _____

Key: (electromagnetic) wave/electromagnetic radiation/photons

39. _____ particles have the strongest ionizing ability and weakest penetrating ability.

Key: Alpha

40. Background radiation counting is not constant, means that radioactivity

A is spontaneous

B is dangerous

C is random

D is weak

Key: C

41. Transformer can induce current in the secondary coil when the primary coil produces a _____

Key: changing magnetic field

42. To reverse the direction of induced e.m.f., direction of _____ can be reversed.

Key: cutting/relative motion/magnetic field

43. The density of electric/magnetic field lines presents the _____ of field.

Key: strength

44. Electrons flow in the _____ direction to the conventional current.

Key: opposite

45. Thermistor's resistance _____ as temperature increases.

Key: decreases/lowers

46. Resistance of a conducting wire is inversely proportional to the _____ of the wire.

Key: (cross-sectional) area

47. Lenz's law states that the current induced in the coil always _____ the change which produced it.

Key: oppose/resist

48. Accretion is a process of interstellar dust and gas gathering by the pull of _____

Key: gravity/gravitational force

49. Red shift is observed when the source is _____ relative to the observer.

Key: moving away
